Architects and Gardeners : November 11th, 2022

There are apparently two types of fiction writers: architects and gardeners.
Architects make plot outlines and thrive on writing with structure.
Gardeners create full characters and personalities, plop them into a universe,
and see what the characters would do, but don’t necessarily know what the end plot
would look like.

I think these two styles can be applied to living our own lives.
We all set goals for ourselves and live in structure, just like an architect would
write a plot. We also all live like gardeners: exploring, progressing,
living day-to-day, and seeing what happens next (living serendipitously).

I’ve found that balancing architecture and gardening into a flexible structure of a
strategy has been optimal for me, so I’m writing this essay to share.
